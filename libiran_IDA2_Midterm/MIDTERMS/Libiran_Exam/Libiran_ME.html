Ramen History
From its humble beginnings in China to becoming Japan's beloved comfort food, ramen has traveled a long way. Introduced in the late 1800s, it became a staple in Japan after World War II, offering warmth and flavor to all. Today, every bowl of ramen tells a story of tradition, creativity, and passion. Whether you like it spicy, savory, or with extra umami, ramen is more than just a meal—it's an experience. Come taste history in every bite.

How to make a delicous osaka ramen
Prepare the Broth In a large pot, combine the chicken stock and pork broth (or extra chicken stock). Bring to a simmer. Add minced garlic, ginger slices, and sesame oil. Let it simmer for about 15-20 minutes to infuse the flavors. Stir in soy sauce, mirin, and miso paste (if using). Taste and adjust seasoning—add more soy sauce for saltiness or more miso for depth.
While the broth is simmering, bring a pot of water to a boil. Cook the ramen noodles according to the package instructions (usually 2-3 minutes for fresh noodles). Once cooked, drain and set aside. Optionally, rinse with cold water to stop the cooking process.
Cook the Noodles Assemble the Ramen Divide the cooked noodles between two bowls. Pour the hot broth over the noodles.
Add the Toppings Place a slice of chashu pork on top of the noodles. Add a soft-boiled egg (cut in half), menma, and green onions. Optionally, sprinkle with nori flakes and a drizzle of chili oil for extra flavor and heat.
Serve Serve immediately, and enjoy your delicious Osaka-style ramen with a rich, savory broth and perfect toppings.
